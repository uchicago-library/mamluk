Here's a list of duplicates found in the secondary authors list. I'm sure I missed some, and as we fix other issues more will come to light. Some of these are probably not duplicates but looked like they could be. You'll have to choose what will be the proper spelling based on OCLC in cases where it's not just a typo.

Wherever there is a single / between multiple authors it needs to be changed to double //. I suspect your semicolons separating authors will also have to become //. Sorry.

To see the full list (if it's not showing up for you in the data entry interface) you'll have to go to the bibliography without being signed in (maybe it'll work in a separate tab or different browser--I don't know how the cookies are set up).

There are probably lots of duplicates in the primary author browse list too, but I can't stand to look any longer.

If some characters are showing up as boxes or nonsense, such as the first letter in the first item in this list, it's because they were entered as html instead of using Alt-Latin. We think that's going to be fixed automatically, and it might not even be visible to you in the data entry interface. Don't worry about it. Yet.
